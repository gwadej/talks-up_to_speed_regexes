Up to Speed with Regexes

G. Wade Johnson

You've heard the one about...

Some people, when confronted with a problem, think I know, I'll use regular expressions. Now they have two problems.
-- Some random crank

The originator of this particular version of the joke was actually Jamie Zawinski. The quote was exhaustively researched by Jeffrey Friedl. Follow the link for more than you wanted to know about the subject.

What are Regular Expressions?

  • A family of languages for matching strings
  • A system for describing strings abstractly
notes

What are they good for?

  • Recognizing strings
  • Parsing strings
  • Extracting strings
  • Transforming strings
  • Validating strings
notes

... Or More succinctly

(Recogniz|Pars|Extract|Transform|Validat)ing strings
notes

Terms

  • Literals: a, %, 3, etc.
  • \0, \n, \r, \t, \f, etc.
  • .
  • \., \*, etc.
  • Anchors/assertions: ^, $
notes

Basic Operations

  • Concatenation: abc
  • Alternation: a|b
  • Quantifiers: a+, a*, a?
notes

What can we do with just this?

  • /wade/
  • /cat|dog/
  • /"..."/
  • /^.+$/
  • /^.*$/
notes

A little more Power: Character Sets

  • \d, \w, \s
  • [aeiou], [0-9], [a-zA-Z0-9_]
  • \D, \W, \S
  • [^aeiou], [^0-9], [^a-zA-Z0-9_]
notes

More Anchors

  • \A
  • \z, \Z
  • \b, \B
notes

Capturing

  • (abc)
  • (?:abc)
  • \1, \2, \3, etc.
notes

More examples

  • /(\d\d\d) \d\d\d/
  • /(\d\d\d) \1/
  • /dead\bbeef/
  • /^\d+$/
  • /\A\d+\z/
  • /\A\d+\Z/
notes

Tips

  • Match what you know
  • Left-most longest match
  • Anchor where you can
  • Anchors match between characters
  • Don't over match
  • Character classes always match a character
  • Consider * or + or ?
notes

Traps

  • Backtracking
  • Readability
  • Efficiency
  • Alternation
  • Matching too much
  • Matching nothing
notes

More Traps

  • /[\s\t\r\n]/
  • /[a-z-]/
  • /[][]/
  • /[.*?+]/
notes

Backtracking and Longest Match

  • Expression: /a.*bc/
  • String: abca123456789abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwzyx
  • Match: abca123456789abc
notes

Readability

  • Shorter is better
  • Less complicated is better
  • Explicit is better
notes

Efficiency

  • Anchors are good: /^\s+key:\s*(.*)/
  • Explicit strings are good: /cat\w*/
  • Nested quantifiers can be dangerous: /(\w*\s*)+/
notes

Alternation

  • Slow: /C\w*\.com|c\w*\.com/
  • Precedence: /^cat|dog$/
notes

Matching Too Much

  • /<p>.+</p>/
  • /<p>[^<]+</p>/
notes

Matching Nothing

Remember a* and a? are successful when they match nothing.
